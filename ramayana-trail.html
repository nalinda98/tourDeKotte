Indo–Lanka Ramayana Trail

A sacred cycling journey connecting heritage, faith, and communities

Trail Overview

Cycling through the living Ramayana landscape

The Indo–Lanka Ramayana Trail is a long-distance cultural and spiritual cycling initiative that traces sacred locations connected to the Ramayana across Sri Lanka and India. Designed as a slow tourism journey, the trail promotes cultural diplomacy, environmental responsibility, and community-based tourism.

This initiative is led by Tour de Kotte as a people-driven movement that blends faith, heritage, endurance cycling, and national unity.

Sri Lanka Route

From the northern shores to the historic capital

  • Kankesanthurai – Entry point of the Ramayana narrative
  • Jaffna & Northern Heritage Villages
  • Cultural & Temple Sites across the island
  • Central Highlands & Ancient Kingdom regions
  • Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte – Historic capital & conclusion

*Route segments are designed for phased and community-supported rides.

Core Objectives

More than a cycling expedition

Cultural Diplomacy

Strengthening Indo–Sri Lankan cultural ties through shared heritage.

Green Tourism

Promoting low-carbon, non-motorised pilgrimage tourism.

Community Livelihoods

Creating opportunities for local guides, homestays, and SMEs.

Endurance & Unity

Using cycling as a symbol of discipline, peace, and coexistence.

Institutional Collaboration

Working with public and cultural institutions

The Ramayana Trail is designed to collaborate with tourism authorities, cultural institutions, diplomatic missions, and reconciliation-focused organisations to ensure authenticity, inclusivity, and long-term sustainability.
